Postcard

If I move to green grass and tall trees
or to the rhythm of ocean and sand,
I will miss the lizard on wire-thin toes
the tail-bobbing roadrunner who is never caught
I'll miss the white saguaro crowns of May,
prickly pear blooming yellow or fuschia
I'll miss the ocotillo bending the wind,
thorns and leaves turned green,
red-orange as the lipstick
my mother wore
